Skint and starving by midday? Burapa’s new lunch set has you covered. 

The eastern-Thai spot on Sukhumvit 11 lays on a daily set menu from 11am-2pm, and at B550 a head, or B530 if you’re going veggie, you get a full sweep of eastern-Thai cooking, plated with a glossy modern finish but rooted firmly in the flavours of Thailand’s coastal provinces.

Each set draws on the cooking of Thailand’s eastern provinces, plated with a modern touch. Take your pick from rice topped with fried seabass and assorted condiments, served with Siamese cardamom chicken soup and pickled papaya salad; Trat’s pad thai with a grilled giant tiger prawn and crispy lemongrass chicken wings; railway fried rice with grilled red chicken, chicken rawang curry and a pork jowl cucumber salad; a shrimp creamy omelette over rice; or eastern spicy crab fried rice with a clear seabass tom yum. Veggie diners get fried tofu with Siamese cardamom mushroom soup or a Trat-style pad thai with crispy mushroom and fried spring rolls.

Fancy a tipple? The drink and snack pairing (B380, daily midday-2.30pm and 5pm-7pm) bags you one cocktail and one small bite. The cocktail list pulls from the full moon bussaba series – the quince, honey-ginger and pomegranate numbers are all worth a punt – or you can stick to the classics. 

To nibble, take your pick from crispy three-flavour chicken with prawn crackers, grilled pork jowl yum yai salad or fried beef tongue naem. It’s a neat little set-up whether you’re rounding off lunch or easing your way towards an early evening.

Open daily, midday-3pm and 5pm-11pm. Book on 02 012 1423 or burapa_reservation@sritrat.com.
