HM King Rama VI wrote more than 20 plays in English after falling for theatre during his years at Oxford. Almost none have ever been staged professionally – until this July.

The King's Command, a one-act farce written by His Majesty around 1901, finally gets its world premiere by Siam Repertory Theatre, led by producer Paul Ewing. The plot: a masked ball, a case of mistaken identity and a royal marriage order.

It's all in service of a big ambition: keeping a century-old royal legacy alive on Bangkok's stage.

Free to attend, first come, first served – July 18 at the Sri Ayudhaya Auditorium, Rama 6 Foundation, National Library.
