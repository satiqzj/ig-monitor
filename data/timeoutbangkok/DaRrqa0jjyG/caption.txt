The trouble with @khaogroup is you make peace with the fact you can't order everything. And what a menu it is. Thick, glossy and stacked with photographs so tempting they only sharpen the agony of choosing. The fried rice alone drags you back, again and again. Khao means 'rice', after all.

This is the Thai restaurant that holds one Michelin star, and has done for five years running. It honours the country's culinary heritage through refined takes on old recipes and premium ingredients sourced close to home. The kitchen fusses over detail like it's a matter of national pride, and honestly, it is. Signature plates sit alongside seasonal specials, each one showing off the depth and swagger of the nation's cooking. Tradition meets a bit of contemporary polish, and nobody loses.

You find it in two Bangkok spots, Ekkamai and Wireless Road, both dressed for locals and visitors chasing one of the city's most decorated tables.

Open 5.30pm-10pm at both branches. Book at www.khaorestaurants.com, or ring Ekkamai on 098 829 8878 and Wireless Road on 097 907 7777.
