A hundred-odd years old, the colonial mansion in Sathorn stands a two-minute stroll from Surasak BTS, all dark teak, high ceilings and starched white linen. Downstairs there's a cooking school running morning classes where you shop the market first. Upstairs, the dining room, where the green curry and massaman come out rich and properly spiced. This is @blueelephant, and it has served heritage Thai cuisine here for decades.

Master Chef Nooror Somany Steppe runs the kitchen. She opened the very first Blue Elephant in Brussels in 1980 and she's been pounding curry paste since she was nine, so you get the idea: she's the real deal.

Bangkok claims two outposts, one in Sathorn and one on Sukhumvit; Phuket keeps another; and the name travels across Europe too. The standard never wavers, wherever you sit down.
