75 years is a long time to spend perfecting the wine. @penfolds knows this better than most. This month, the Australian house brought its most famous wine, Grange, to Thailand to mark three-quarters of a century of quiet obsession, gathering collectors and sommeliers over glasses poured with real ceremony.

Grange isn't the product of one vineyard but many, sourced across regions and stitched together through the sort of blending Penfolds has spent decades refining. Max Schubert, the house's first chief winemaker, dreamed it up in 1951, determined to make an Australian red bold enough to age like the grand old bottles of Europe and outlive everyone who doubted him.

He was right. Grange has since collected perfect scores from the world's fussiest critics on more than 37 occasions, which says less about luck than about craft, patience and a refusal to cut corners.

So here's to a wine that spent 75 years earning its reputation. Few bottles ever come close.
