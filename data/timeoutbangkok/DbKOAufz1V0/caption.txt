Japan has always known that craft and play aren’t opposites. The Superlative Artistry of Japan makes the case beautifully, gathering Japanese arts from the Meiji era to now, and after touring the world since 2017 it finally comes to Thailand.

Start in the 19th century with ‘Kogei’, the handiwork that once floored Westerners. There’s a giant ‘Satsuma vase’ finished in gold leaf, and a hanging decoration from Nikko Shrine worked from tens of thousands of silk threads, one at a time. Then comes the twist. That same obsession carries through to the contemporary section – the uncannily ‘realistic food models’, a ‘cabbage-leaf bowl’ you want to wash, and 100-plus ‘tiny animal figures’ from Kaiyodo. Same devotion, different century.

Entry is free. It tours Thailand across 2026 as follows:
Bangkok: July 25-August 23, 2026. First floor, TCDC Bangkok, 10.30am-7pm, closed Mondays.

Khon Kaen: September 1-30, 2026. First floor, TCDC Khon Kaen, 10.30-7pm.

Chiang Mai: October 10-November 15, 2026. First floor, TCDC Chiang Mai, 10.30-7pm.
